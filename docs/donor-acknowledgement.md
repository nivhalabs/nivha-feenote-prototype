## How to Use This Document (Staff Guidance — Not Shown to the Donor)

This document contains the GDPR wording to be added to the legal (medico-legal) collection form, as identified in the NIVHA GDPR Implementation Plan (item 5.3). It is designed for three uses:

1. **The current online legal collection form** — add Sections A–E below as form sections, with the declarations as required checkboxes and signature fields.
2. **Paper collection forms** — print Sections A–E as the final page of the collection pack, signed at collection.
3. **The new fee note tool collection module** — when donor-facing collection screens are built, reuse this wording verbatim.

Rules for collectors:

- Sections A–D must be read with the donor, and agreement to sampling confirmed, **before the sample is taken**, at every collection, with no exceptions.
- The donor signs **once**, immediately after sealing, at the same moment as the chain of custody paperwork — one combined signature covers identity, agreement to sampling, the data notice, and the medication disclosures (Section E). Do not ask for a second, separate GDPR signature.
- The donor must be offered the NIVHA medico-legal privacy notice (paper copy or the link/QR code) **before** signing. Record their choice — do not skip this step if they decline a copy; offering it is what matters.
- Never tell a donor their processing is "based on consent" for Trust or solicitor instructions — it is not (see Section C). Their signature records **agreement to sampling** and confirmation of the information given, not a GDPR lawful basis.
- If the donor refuses to sign or refuses sampling, do not collect. Record the refusal and notify the instructing party — see Section C wording, which tells the donor this will happen.
- For donors under 18, the parent/guardian section is mandatory: written consent and presence of a parent or guardian. If neither is available, do not collect.
- For **private clients** (self-instructed, personal reassurance), use the alternative Section C-P instead of Section C. The rest of the form is identical.

## Section B — What Happens to Your Sample and Your Information

In plain terms:

- Your identity is verified and recorded now, at collection, along with the date, time, and place of collection and the details of the sample taken.
- Your sample is sealed in front of you with tamper-evident seals and given a unique reference. This is called chain of custody — it is how the laboratory and the court can trust that the sample tested is yours.
- Your sample is sent to an accredited laboratory for analysis. The results come back to NIVHA.
- Test results are health information. The law treats them as special category data and gives them extra protection.
- The results, and any expert report, are provided to the instructing organisation named in Section A. In legal proceedings, reports may also be provided to the court and to other parties in the proceedings, as the court directs.
- We never sell your information. We never share your results with anyone outside the people described above, except where the law requires it.
- Details you gave when the test was arranged are not reused for any other case, and returning clients are never shown your details.

Full details — including how long we keep records, the secure systems we use, and the safeguards for any data stored outside the UK — are in the **NIVHA privacy notice for medico-legal testing**, which is available now from your collector as a paper copy, and online at any time.

## Section C — Your Rights and the Legal Basis (Trust and Solicitor Instructions)

**The legal basis for this testing is not your consent.** The testing has been instructed in connection with legal proceedings or the instructing organisation's duties towards children and families. Data protection law allows this processing because it is necessary in connection with legal claims and, where children are involved, for safeguarding purposes.

Because the legal basis is not consent:

- You cannot withdraw consent to the processing of the test result once the sample has been analysed and reported; and
- Some rights, such as erasure, are limited while records are needed for the proceedings.

**However, we will not take a sample without your agreement.** You may decline to provide a sample now. If you decline, no sample will be taken, and we will record your decision and inform the instructing organisation, who may report it to the court. What that means for your case is a matter for the instructing organisation and the court — not NIVHA.

You keep all of your other data protection rights, including the right to a copy of the personal data NIVHA holds about you (a subject access request), the rights to rectification and restriction, and the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (or the Data Protection Commission in the Republic of Ireland). The privacy notice explains how to use them. For questions about your case, your first point of contact is the instructing organisation named in Section A.

If your result is positive and a B sample (a second, sealed portion of your sample) has been retained, you may ask for it to be tested at an independent accredited laboratory. The privacy notice and your instructing organisation can explain how.

## Section C-P — Your Consent (Private Clients Only)

*Use this section instead of Section C where the client has instructed and paid for their own test.*

You have instructed and paid for this test yourself. The legal basis for processing your test results (which are special category health data) is your **explicit consent**.

- You may withdraw your consent at any time **before analysis is complete** by emailing info@nivha.net. If you withdraw, testing stops; work already performed remains chargeable under our terms of engagement.
- Once analysis is complete and your result has been reported to you, withdrawal no longer applies to the work already done, but you keep all your other rights, including erasure where no legal obligation requires us to keep the record.
- Your results are reported to you alone. We do not share them with anyone else unless you ask us to in writing, or the law requires it.

## Section D — Medication and Product Use

Some prescribed medicines, over-the-counter remedies, foods, and cosmetic products can affect test results. Telling us about them helps the laboratory and the reporting scientist interpret your result fairly and accurately.

You do not have to disclose anything, but anything you do disclose will be recorded on the collection form and shared with the laboratory and reporting scientist, and may appear in the report.
