## 1. Background and Recitals

(A) The Instructing Organisation instructs NIVHA to perform drug and/or alcohol testing in connection with family or care proceedings, other legal proceedings, or the Instructing Organisation's statutory duties towards children and families (the "Instructed Testing").

(B) Testing is instructed on a case-by-case basis by way of a fee note raised through NIVHA's fee note tool, or by written instruction, in each case identifying the donor, the panels and sample types required, and the applicable timeframes (each an "Instruction").

(C) In the course of delivering the Instructed Testing, both Parties process personal data — including special category data (data concerning health) — as independent controllers. Each Party determines the purposes and means of its own processing, and this Agreement governs the data sharing arrangements between them.

(D) In Trust and solicitor instructions, neither Party relies on the consent of the donor as its lawful basis for processing. The legal foundation for processing is set out in clause 5 and the applicable route schedule. Donor agreement to sampling is nonetheless always obtained at the point of collection, as recorded in the donor acknowledgement (see clause 6.4).

(E) This Agreement is supplemental to any services contract, framework agreement, or letter of instruction between the Parties (the "Services Contract") and sets out the data protection obligations of the Parties in respect of the personal data shared between them.

## 3. Controller Responsibilities

3.1 Each Party is an independent controller in respect of the personal data it processes under and in connection with this Agreement. Neither Party processes personal data on behalf of the other Party as a processor.

3.2 Each Party shall:

- Comply with all obligations applicable to it under Applicable Data Protection Law;
- Maintain a record of processing activities under Article 30;
- Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (Article 32);
- Ensure that any person authorised to process personal data has committed themselves to confidentiality or is under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;
- Conduct data protection impact assessments where required under Article 35.

3.3 Where the Parties jointly determine the purposes and means of any specific processing activity, they shall enter into a joint controller arrangement in accordance with Article 26 and update this Agreement accordingly. *(Review point: for Trust instructions in ongoing care proceedings, the controller/joint-controller determination should be confirmed by legal advice — see clause 16.)*

## 5. Lawful Basis for Sharing

5.1 The lawful bases applicable to each route are set out in Schedule 1 (Trust instructions) and Schedule 2 (Solicitor instructions). In both routes:

- Test results, medication disclosures, and related records are Special Category Data, and both Parties shall identify and document an Article 9 condition before processing;
- Neither Party relies on the consent of the donor as its lawful basis for processing (without prejudice to donor agreement to sampling at collection, and save for any private-paying arrangement which falls outside this Agreement);
- Each Party shall record its lawful bases in its record of processing activities.

5.2 Where the donor is resident in the Republic of Ireland or the processing otherwise falls within the scope of EU law, the equivalent EU GDPR provisions apply.

## 6. Transparency Obligations

6.1 The Instructing Organisation shall inform the donor about NIVHA's involvement in the testing process before collection takes place, including providing or making available NIVHA's privacy notice (Medico-Legal Drug and Alcohol Testing) or directing the donor to it. This discharges the Article 14 information obligations arising from the Instructing Organisation providing donor details to NIVHA.

6.2 NIVHA shall provide its privacy notice (Medico-Legal Drug and Alcohol Testing) to the donor at the point of sample collection, or make it available electronically, and shall record this in the donor acknowledgement.

6.3 Each Party is responsible for maintaining its own privacy notice in compliance with Articles 13 and 14 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

6.4 NIVHA shall obtain a completed donor acknowledgement at every collection, recording identity verification, agreement to sampling, medication and product-use disclosures, and confirmation that the privacy notice was provided or offered. For donors under 18, a parent or guardian must consent and be present, and the acknowledgement shall record this.

## 8. Security Measures

8.1 Each Party shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, in accordance with Article 32 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

8.2 Such measures shall include, as appropriate:

- Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest;
- Role-based access controls with the principle of least privilege;
- Multi-factor authentication for systems holding personal data;
- Regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of security measures;
- Staff training on data protection and information security;
- Physical security of premises where personal data is processed;
- Secure disposal of physical records containing personal data.

8.3 Instruction details and donor details shall be transferred between the Parties only via encrypted channels — the NIVHA fee note tool (served over HTTPS), encrypted email, or a secure portal. Transmission of donor details by unencrypted email is prohibited.

8.4 Each Party shall notify the other Party promptly if it becomes aware of any material change to its security measures that may affect the protection of shared personal data.

## 9. Retention

9.1 Each Party is responsible for determining and applying its own retention schedule in accordance with Applicable Data Protection Law and any applicable statutory, regulatory, or court-directed requirements.

9.2 NIVHA's retention schedule for medico-legal records is as follows:

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Test results, scientific reports, and chain of custody documentation | 6 years from the date of the final report on the Instruction |
| Fee notes and case administration records | 6 years from the date of the final report on the Instruction |
| Donor acknowledgements | Aligned with the corresponding test records |
| Secure sign-in and account data | 12 months from last activity |

*(Review point: retention for records used in ongoing family or care proceedings may need to extend until the conclusion of proceedings, including any appeal. To be confirmed with legal advice.)*

9.3 The Instructing Organisation shall confirm its own retention obligations in writing, having regard to court rules, Trust records management policy, or Law Society guidance as applicable.

9.4 At the end of the applicable retention period, each Party shall securely delete or anonymise the relevant personal data.

## 10. Breach Notification

10.1 Each Party shall notify the other Party of any Personal Data Breach affecting shared personal data within 24 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

10.2 The notification shall include, to the extent known:

- The nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected;
- The likely consequences of the breach;
- The measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects;
- The name and contact details of the person responsible for managing the breach.

10.3 Each Party is independently responsible for:

- Assessing whether the breach meets the threshold for notification to the relevant Supervisory Authority under Article 33 (within 72 hours of awareness);
- Notifying the Supervisory Authority where required;
- Communicating the breach to affected data subjects where required under Article 34.

10.4 The Parties shall cooperate in good faith in investigating and remediating any breach affecting shared personal data. Where a breach affects records before the court, the Parties shall also consider any notification obligations to the court.

## 11. Data Subject Rights and SAR Handling

11.1 Each Party shall handle data subject requests (including Subject Access Requests) in respect of personal data it holds, in accordance with Articles 15–22 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

11.2 **NIVHA's signposting policy:** Where a donor contacts NIVHA with a data subject request, NIVHA's policy is to signpost the donor to the Instructing Organisation in the first instance, as the Instructing Organisation holds the primary case relationship and case file. This does not relieve NIVHA of its obligation to respond to formal Subject Access Requests for the personal data NIVHA itself holds, within one calendar month.

11.3 Where a data subject request received by one Party relates to personal data held by the other Party, the receiving Party shall promptly inform the other Party and the Parties shall cooperate to ensure a timely and complete response.

11.4 Because processing under this Agreement is not based on consent, there is no right to withdraw consent, and the rights to erasure and portability are limited in scope. Both Parties shall ensure that their privacy notices explain this clearly, and shall consider any restriction on disclosure arising from the proceedings themselves (including rules on disclosure of documents filed in family proceedings) before responding to any request.

## 12. B Sample Facilitation

12.1 Where the collection protocol and sample format permit, NIVHA shall retain a B sample (split or secondary sample) in accordance with chain of custody requirements.

12.2 If a donor's test result is positive and a B sample has been retained, the donor may request that the B sample be analysed by an independent laboratory. Such analysis shall be at the requesting party's own expense unless otherwise agreed or directed by the court.

12.3 The Instructing Organisation shall inform donors of their B sample rights where applicable, and NIVHA shall cooperate in facilitating any B sample analysis request, including the secure transfer of the B sample to the nominated independent laboratory.

## 13. Court Disclosure

13.1 Nothing in this Agreement prevents either Party from disclosing personal data where required by a court order, witness summons, or rule of court. Where practicable and lawful, the disclosing Party shall inform the other Party before making such a disclosure.

13.2 Scientific reports prepared for proceedings are expert evidence, and their use and onward disclosure within proceedings is governed by the rules of court and the directions of the court.

## 14. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

14.2 The Parties irrevocably submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Northern Ireland in respect of any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement.
