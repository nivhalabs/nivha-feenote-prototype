## 1. Acceptance and Scope

1.1 These Data Sharing Terms (the "Terms") apply between **NIVHA Laboratory Services Ltd** ("NIVHA") and each organisation that instructs NIVHA to perform medico-legal drug and/or alcohol testing — a Health and Social Care Trust or a firm of solicitors (the "Instructing Organisation").

1.2 The Instructing Organisation accepts these Terms:

- by ticking the acceptance box when submitting an instruction through the NIVHA fee note tool — the accepted version, the date and time of acceptance, and the accepting individual's details are recorded against the client case number (CCN); or
- by issuing a written instruction to NIVHA, in which case these Terms are incorporated by reference and apply from the date of the instruction.

1.3 The individual accepting these Terms confirms that they are authorised to do so on behalf of the Instructing Organisation.

1.4 These Terms do not apply to private individuals who commission testing for personal reassurance. Private instructions are governed by NIVHA's private client terms and privacy notice, and are based on the client's explicit consent.

1.5 A countersigned agreement containing these terms is available on request for organisations whose information governance procedures require an executed document — contact andy@nivha.net.

1.6 The version of these Terms in force at the date of each instruction applies to that instruction. NIVHA publishes the current version at all times and identifies changes by version number and date.

## 2. Background

2.1 The Instructing Organisation instructs NIVHA to perform drug and/or alcohol testing in connection with family or care proceedings, other legal proceedings, or the Instructing Organisation's statutory duties towards children and families (the "Instructed Testing").

2.2 Testing is instructed on a case-by-case basis by way of a fee note raised through NIVHA's fee note tool, or by written instruction, in each case identifying the donor, the panels and sample types required, and the applicable timeframes (each an "Instruction").

2.3 In delivering the Instructed Testing, both parties process personal data — including special category data (data concerning health) — as independent controllers. Each party determines the purposes and means of its own processing, and these Terms govern the data sharing arrangements between them.

2.4 In Trust and solicitor instructions, neither party relies on the consent of the donor as its lawful basis for processing. The legal foundation for processing is set out in section 6 and the applicable route section (12 or 13). Donor agreement to sampling is nonetheless always obtained at the point of collection, as recorded in the donor acknowledgement (see section 7.4).

2.5 These Terms are supplemental to any services contract, framework agreement, or letter of instruction between the parties, and set out the data protection obligations of the parties in respect of the personal data shared between them.

## 4. Controller Responsibilities

4.1 Each party is an independent controller in respect of the personal data it processes under and in connection with these Terms. Neither party processes personal data on behalf of the other as a processor.

4.2 Each party shall:

- Comply with all obligations applicable to it under Applicable Data Protection Law;
- Maintain a record of processing activities under Article 30;
- Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk (Article 32);
- Ensure that any person authorised to process personal data has committed themselves to confidentiality or is under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;
- Conduct data protection impact assessments where required under Article 35.

4.3 Where the parties jointly determine the purposes and means of any specific processing activity, they shall enter into a joint controller arrangement in accordance with Article 26. *(Review point: for Trust instructions in ongoing care proceedings, the controller/joint-controller determination should be confirmed by legal advice — see section 16.)*

| Data Category | Description |

5.4 Results and scientific reports are shared only with the designated case contacts within the Instructing Organisation identified on the Instruction, and are never disclosed by NIVHA to any other client or third party except as set out in these Terms or as directed by the court.

## 6. Lawful Basis for Sharing

6.1 The lawful bases applicable to each route are set out in section 12 (Trust instructions) and section 13 (Solicitor instructions). In both routes:

- Test results, medication disclosures, and related records are Special Category Data, and both parties shall identify and document an Article 9 condition before processing;
- Neither party relies on the consent of the donor as its lawful basis for processing (without prejudice to donor agreement to sampling at collection);
- Each party shall record its lawful bases in its record of processing activities.

6.2 Where the donor is resident in the Republic of Ireland or the processing otherwise falls within the scope of EU law, the equivalent EU GDPR provisions apply.

## 7. Transparency Obligations

7.1 The Instructing Organisation shall inform the donor about NIVHA's involvement in the testing process before collection takes place, including providing or making available NIVHA's privacy notice (Medico-Legal Drug and Alcohol Testing) or directing the donor to it. This discharges the Article 14 information obligations arising from the Instructing Organisation providing donor details to NIVHA.

7.2 NIVHA shall provide its privacy notice (Medico-Legal Drug and Alcohol Testing) to the donor at the point of sample collection, or make it available electronically, and shall record this in the donor acknowledgement.

7.3 Each party is responsible for maintaining its own privacy notice in compliance with Articles 13 and 14 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

7.4 NIVHA shall obtain a completed donor acknowledgement at every collection, recording identity verification, agreement to sampling, medication and product-use disclosures, and confirmation that the privacy notice was provided or offered. For donors under 18, a parent or guardian must consent and be present, and the acknowledgement shall record this.

## 9. Security Measures

9.1 Each party shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing and against accidental loss, destruction, or damage, in accordance with Article 32 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

9.2 Such measures shall include, as appropriate:

- Encryption of personal data in transit and at rest;
- Role-based access controls with the principle of least privilege;
- Multi-factor authentication for systems holding personal data;
- Regular testing, assessment, and evaluation of security measures;
- Staff training on data protection and information security;
- Physical security of premises where personal data is processed;
- Secure disposal of physical records containing personal data.

9.3 Instruction details and donor details shall be transferred between the parties only via encrypted channels — the NIVHA fee note tool (served over HTTPS), encrypted email, or a secure portal. Transmission of donor details by unencrypted email is prohibited.

9.4 Each party shall notify the other promptly if it becomes aware of any material change to its security measures that may affect the protection of shared personal data.

## 10. Retention

10.1 Each party is responsible for determining and applying its own retention schedule in accordance with Applicable Data Protection Law and any applicable statutory, regulatory, or court-directed requirements.

10.2 NIVHA's retention schedule for medico-legal records is as follows:

| Record Type | Retention Period |
|---|---|
| Test results, scientific reports, and chain of custody documentation | 6 years from the date of the final report on the Instruction |
| Fee notes and case administration records | 6 years from the date of the final report on the Instruction |
| Donor acknowledgements | Aligned with the corresponding test records |
| Secure sign-in and account data | 12 months from last activity |

*(Review point: retention for records used in ongoing family or care proceedings may need to extend until the conclusion of proceedings, including any appeal. To be confirmed with legal advice.)*

10.3 The Instructing Organisation is responsible for its own retention obligations, having regard to court rules, Trust records management policy, or Law Society guidance as applicable.

10.4 At the end of the applicable retention period, each party shall securely delete or anonymise the relevant personal data.

## 11. Breach Notification

11.1 Each party shall notify the other of any Personal Data Breach affecting shared personal data within 24 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

11.2 The notification shall include, to the extent known:

- The nature of the breach, including the categories and approximate number of data subjects affected;
- The likely consequences of the breach;
- The measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects;
- The name and contact details of the person responsible for managing the breach.

11.3 Each party is independently responsible for:

- Assessing whether the breach meets the threshold for notification to the relevant Supervisory Authority under Article 33 (within 72 hours of awareness);
- Notifying the Supervisory Authority where required;
- Communicating the breach to affected data subjects where required under Article 34.

11.4 The parties shall cooperate in good faith in investigating and remediating any breach affecting shared personal data. Where a breach affects records before the court, the parties shall also consider any notification obligations to the court.

## 12. Trust Instructions (Route Section)

**Applies where the Instructing Organisation is a Health and Social Care Trust.**

12.1 The Trust instructs testing in connection with care proceedings or its statutory duties towards children and families under the Children (Northern Ireland) Order 1995 and associated legislation.

12.2 Lawful bases for the Trust sharing data with NIVHA:

- **Article 6(1)(e) — Public task:** sharing is necessary for the performance of the Trust's statutory functions in relation to the care and protection of children and families;
- **Article 6(1)(c) — Legal obligation:** where testing is directed by the court;
- **Article 9(2)(f) — Legal claims / judicial acts:** processing of special category data is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims, or whenever courts are acting in their judicial capacity;
- **DPA 2018 Schedule 1 Part 2 paragraph 18 — Safeguarding:** where applicable, processing is necessary for the protection of children.

12.3 Lawful bases for NIVHA sharing results with the Trust:

- **Article 6(1)(e) — Public task:** the sharing supports the Trust's statutory functions, which NIVHA supports as instructed testing provider;
- **Article 9(2)(f) — Legal claims / judicial acts;**
- **DPA 2018 Schedule 1 Part 2 paragraph 18 — Safeguarding**, where applicable.

12.4 Where a Trust's information governance procedures require a countersigned agreement, controller assurance documentation, or additional terms, NIVHA will complete these on request (see section 1.5).

## 13. Solicitor Instructions (Route Section)

**Applies where the Instructing Organisation is a firm of solicitors.**

13.1 The firm instructs testing on behalf of a client in connection with family or other legal proceedings, including legally aided instructions. Where the instruction is legally aided, the applicable legal aid authority's rates are observed, and the legal aid reference is processed as part of the Instruction.

13.2 Lawful bases for the firm sharing data with NIVHA:

- **Article 6(1)(b) — Contract:** sharing is necessary for the performance of the firm's contract with NIVHA and to take steps prior to entering that contract;
- **Article 6(1)(f) — Legitimate interests:** in respect of the firm's client's interest in obtaining expert evidence, balanced against the rights of the donor;
- **Article 9(2)(f) — Legal claims / judicial acts:** processing of special category data is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

13.3 Lawful bases for NIVHA sharing results with the firm:

- **Article 6(1)(b) — Contract:** returning results and reports to the instructing firm is the core performance of NIVHA's contract;
- **Article 9(2)(f) — Legal claims / judicial acts.**

13.4 The firm confirms it will handle results and reports in accordance with its professional obligations, including confidentiality and legal professional privilege, and will only use them for the purposes of the matter on which testing was instructed.

## 14. Data Subject Rights and SAR Handling

14.1 Each party shall handle data subject requests (including Subject Access Requests) in respect of personal data it holds, in accordance with Articles 15–22 of the Applicable Data Protection Law.

14.2 **NIVHA's signposting policy:** Where a donor contacts NIVHA with a data subject request, NIVHA's policy is to signpost the donor to the Instructing Organisation in the first instance, as the Instructing Organisation holds the primary case relationship and case file. This does not relieve NIVHA of its obligation to respond to formal Subject Access Requests for the personal data NIVHA itself holds, within one calendar month.

14.3 Where a data subject request received by one party relates to personal data held by the other party, the receiving party shall promptly inform the other and the parties shall cooperate to ensure a timely and complete response.

14.4 Because processing under these Terms is not based on consent, there is no right to withdraw consent, and the rights to erasure and portability are limited in scope. Both parties shall ensure that their privacy notices explain this clearly, and shall consider any restriction on disclosure arising from the proceedings themselves (including rules on disclosure of documents filed in family proceedings) before responding to any request.

## 15. B Sample, Court Disclosure, Governing Law and General

15.1 **B sample:** Where the collection protocol and sample format permit, NIVHA retains a B sample (split or secondary sample) in accordance with chain of custody requirements. If a donor's test result is positive and a B sample has been retained, the donor may request that the B sample be analysed by an independent laboratory, at the requesting party's own expense unless otherwise agreed or directed by the court. The Instructing Organisation shall inform donors of their B sample rights where applicable, and NIVHA shall cooperate in facilitating any B sample analysis request, including the secure transfer of the B sample to the nominated independent laboratory.

15.2 **Court disclosure:** Nothing in these Terms prevents either party from disclosing personal data where required by a court order, witness summons, or rule of court. Where practicable and lawful, the disclosing party shall inform the other before making such a disclosure. Scientific reports prepared for proceedings are expert evidence, and their use and onward disclosure within proceedings is governed by the rules of court and the directions of the court.

15.3 **Governing law:** These Terms and any dispute or claim arising out of or in connection with them are governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Northern Ireland, and the parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Northern Ireland.

15.4 **Term:** These Terms apply for so long as Instructions continue between the parties and survive thereafter to the extent necessary to give effect to the retention and deletion obligations set out herein.

15.5 **Severability:** If any provision of these Terms is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions continue in full force and effect.

15.6 **Entire terms:** These Terms, together with any services contract and each Instruction, constitute the entire agreement between the parties in relation to the data sharing arrangements described herein.
